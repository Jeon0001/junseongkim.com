About

I am a BSc student in the School of Computing at the Korea Advanced Institute of Science and Technology (KAIST), I am also reading Business and Technology Management as a double Major.

My interests lie in the intersection of data science, ethical technology and business ethics.

Before joining KAIST, I was based in the United Kingdom where I completed my K-12 education at Magdalen College School, Oxford where I developed a passion for all things STEM.

I am currently working at the Institute for Basic Science as a collaborative data science intern under the Planetary Atmosphere Group and the Data Science Group.

My current research focuses on processing data obtained by the Akatsuki Venus Orbiter and creating models for anomaly detection to find "Stationary Waves" in Venus' atmosphere.

In my free time, I enjoy playing the guitar, competitive swimming, and cooking.

Please feel free to reach out if you would like to chat about anything!



My Philosophy

I believe that technology should be used to improve the lives of all people and that it should be developed in an ethical and sustainable manner.

I am passionate about using data science to solve the world's most pressing problems and I am always looking for opportunities to use my skills to make a positive impact on the world.

I am also passionate about business ethics and I believe that businesses should be held to the highest ethical standards.

I am always open to new opportunities and I am happy to help out with any projects that align with my interests.
